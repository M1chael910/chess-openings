Queen's Gambit

The Queen's Gambit starts after the moves 1.d4 d5 2. c4

The Queen's Gambit is considered a more strategic opening. Although it isn't without any drawbacks.

Pros Cons
Fights to control the center White may have to spend time getting the pawn back
Puts immediate pressure on black Less attacking chances against the black king
Gains Space Black may aim to counterattack d4

Variations

Slav Defense

The Slav Defense is the most popular variation against 2.c4. Black declines the gambit and reinforces the central d4-pawn with 2...c6. Black avoids locking the light-squared bishop inside the pawn chain like in the Queen's Gambit Declined (discussed below). The Slav Defense has a large amount of theory and is considered a different opening on its own.
